CONTRIBUTIONS

 Grace Hopper was a programming pioneer. She developed the idea of automatic programming and ways of using the computer to code. In 1952, she created a machine known as a compiler called A-0 that could translate mathematical code into machine-readable code. This was a huge breakthrough in computational thinking. Without this innovation, we wouldn’t have access to the computers we have today.

UNDERREPRESENTATION

Grace Hopper faced significant underrepresentation in the tech field mainly due to gender bias. During her time, society often viewed women as less capable in science and technology, leading to their ideas being dismissed or overlooked. Despite her groundbreaking contributions, such as creating the first compiler, many saw her work as unimportant simply because she was a woman. Her innovative approach—using words instead of symbols in programming—was doubted by many. Still, Hopper persevered and proved that her ideas were valuable, showing the strength and brilliance women can bring to the tech world.
